Quick Clinical Facts
🧫
HCV window periodAnti-HCV antibody may be undetectable for 8–12 weeks post-exposure. RNA becomes positive within 1–2 weeks — test RNA if acute infection is suspected.
⚡
SDD means dose harderCefepime SDD requires either 2g q8h extended infusion (4h) or high-dose regimens. Standard 2g q12h will fail PK/PD targets.
🧠
LGI1 encephalitis clueFaciobrachial dystonic seizures (FBDS) are the pathognomonic prodrome — often misdiagnosed as epilepsy before AE workup is considered.
🦠
Occult HBV reactivationScreen all immunosuppression candidates with HBsAg + anti-HBc total. A positive anti-HBc warrants HBV DNA before starting biologics or chemotherapy.
Glossary
PPV — Positive Predictive Value
Probability a positive test means true infection. Falls as prevalence drops — why HCV ELISA positives in low-risk groups need RNA confirmation.
LLOQ
Lower Limit of Quantification. "Detected, below LLOQ" = virus present but unquantifiable. Not the same as undetectable.
SDD
Susceptible-Dose-Dependent. Standard dosing fails. High dose or extended infusion required to achieve pharmacodynamic target.
Seronegative Viraemia
Detectable viral RNA despite non-reactive serology. Occurs in immunosuppression, early acute infection, or assay failure.

SDD means the organism can be treated with cefepime — but only with the right dose and infusion strategy. Standard dosing (2g q12h over 30 min) will fail to reach PK/PD targets. You need either 2g q8h as an extended infusion over 4 hours, or high-dose 2g q8h.

Key pointSDD ≠ Susceptible with standard dosing. In serious infections the difference is clinically significant.

Apply the Graus 2016 criteria: subacute onset (<3 months) of working memory deficits, altered consciousness, or new psychiatric symptoms, plus at least one of: CSF pleocytosis, suggestive MRI, new-onset unexplained seizures, or abnormal EEG. Common infectious and structural causes must be excluded first — AE testing is a second-step investigation, not a first-line screen.

LLOQ (Lower Limit of Quantification) is the minimum viral load a PCR assay can measure accurately and reproducibly. Below this threshold, the virus may still be detected but cannot be reliably counted. "Detected, below LLOQ" means the virus is present — the patient is still viraemic.

Clinical implication"Below LLOQ" ≠ undetectable. Do not reassure the patient that virus is absent. Each assay has its own LLOQ — do not compare numbers across different platforms.
